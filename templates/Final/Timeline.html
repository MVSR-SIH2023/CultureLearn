Evolution of Indian Civilization
01
Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE):
  • Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro civilizations.
  • Advanced urban planning and drainage systems.
Vedic Period (1500–500 BCE):
  • Composition of the Vedas.
  • Emergence of early Hinduism.
02
03
Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE):
  • Chandragupta Maurya and Ashoka the Great.
  • Spread of Buddhism.
Gupta Empire (320–550 CE):
  • Golden age of Indian arts, sciences, and philosophy.
  • Decimal system, concept of zero, and Aryabhata's contributions.
04
05
Medieval Period (600–1600 CE):
  • Islamic invasions and establishment of Delhi Sultanate.
  • Mughal Empire (1526–1857) with notable rulers like Akbar and Shah Jahan.
  • Bhakti and Sufi movements.
Vijayanagara and Bahmani Empires (1336–1646 CE):
  • Flourishing South Indian kingdoms.
  • Hampi as a major cultural and economic center.
06
07
Colonial Period (1600–1947):
  • Arrival of European powers (Portuguese, Dutch, and British).
  • British East India Company and subsequent colonization.
  • Indian Rebellion of 1857.
Independence and Partition (1947):
  • Independence from British rule on August 15, 1947.
  • Partition of India into India and Pakistan.
08
09
Post-Independence Era (1947–present):
  • Formation of the Republic of India.
  • Economic liberalization in 1991.
  • Various political and social developments.
Modern India (21st Century):
  • Continued economic growth and technological advancements.
  • Social changes and challenges.
  • Ongoing political developments.
